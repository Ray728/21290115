STAGE 1

In this photo, we see a prototype of smart glasses scattered across a workbench, capturing the essence of a project in its nascent stage. The idea and inspiration for these glasses sprouted from the worlds of movies and literature, where futuristic gadgets often fuel our imagination.

The process of manually assembling the prototype from scratch presents its fair share of challenges. The initial models are typically built using makeshift materials, and success is far from guaranteed on the first attempt. Each component must be meticulously selected, and in the case of the glasses, finding the perfect lenses became a meticulous task

STAGE 2

Once the necessary components are obtained, the smart glasses development progresses to the prototype assembly stage. First, the glasses' housing is manually assembled with precision to ensure seamless fitting. Next, the surface is aligned using sandpaper to create a smooth and polished appearance. Automotive-grade filler is then applied to prepare the housing for a vinyl coating, removing any remaining rough spots. A carefully chosen vinyl coating is applied, covering the entire surface and enhancing the glasses' professional look. Heat is applied to ensure proper adhesion and to eliminate any wrinkles or imperfections, resulting in a visually pleasing final product. This meticulous process gives the prototype smart glasses their distinctive and marketable appearance. It plays a critical role in preparing the product for further testing, refinement, and eventual mass production.

STAGE 3

The prototype development stage involves several steps to ensure the functionality and performance of the product. Once the components and casing are assembled, the testing phase begins. This involves assessing the prototype's operational capabilities and functionalities and identifying any software errors or minor flaws.

During testing, various experiments and simulations are conducted to evaluate the prototype's performance under different conditions. This helps detect any potential weaknesses or areas for improvement. Any software bugs or glitches are identified and rectified, ensuring a stable and smooth user experience.

STAGE 4

In parallel with the testing process, the packaging for the product is designed and created. This packaging is specifically tailored to accommodate the prototype and protect it during storage, transportation, and sale. The packaging design may reflect the brand identity and provide relevant information about the product.

Once all the necessary adjustments are made and the final version of the prototype is ready, including its refined software and packaging, it is considered ready for sale. This signifies the successful completion of the prototype development stage, with the product now prepared to enter the market.
